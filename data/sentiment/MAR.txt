# Marriott International (MAR) — Company Report
**Date of Analysis: June 17, 2024**

## Company Overview
Marriott International is the world’s largest hotel company by number of rooms, operating over 8,600 properties and 1.6 million rooms across nearly 140 countries under 30 brands. The company’s business model centers on franchising and managing hotels rather than owning them outright, which limits asset risk and enables global scalability.

## Latest Financial Data (Q1 2024, reported May 1, 2024)
- **Revenue:** $5.98 billion (up 6.3% YoY)
- **Net Income:** $700 million (up 12% YoY)
- **EPS:** $2.13 (beat consensus estimates)
- **Systemwide RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room):** +4.2% YoY
- **Adjusted EBITDA:** $1.175 billion (up 7% YoY)
- **Pipeline:** Approximately 557,000 rooms in pipeline as of March 31, 2024

**Stock performance:** MAR shares have risen ~17% year-to-date, generally outperforming the S&P 500 Hotels, Resorts & Cruise Lines sector.

## Key Insights

### Positives
- **Strong Revenue and Profit Growth:** Q1 results exceeded analyst expectations, driven by both higher occupancy and average daily rates (ADR), especially in North America and EMEA regions.
- **Robust Demand Across Segments:** Continued outperformance in leisure travel, with improving trends in group and business travel, especially in the U.S. and Asia Pacific markets.
- **Asset-Light Model:** High return on invested capital with relatively low operational risk, improving cash flow stability.
- **Loyalty Program Strength:** Marriott Bonvoy has over 192 million members, fostering customer engagement and repeat business.
- **Expansion Plans:** Large development pipeline, particularly in China, South Asia, and the Middle East, suggesting solid long-term growth prospects.
- **Shareholder Returns:** Ongoing dividend (annual yield ~1%) and $900 million in share buybacks in Q1 2024.

### Risks & Negatives
- **China Headwinds:** Recovery in China has lagged North America and Europe due to slower-than-expected economic rebound and ongoing travel restrictions.
- **Cost Inflation:** Wage and utility costs continue to rise, pressuring hotel margins, though MAR has managed to offset some increases with price hikes.
- **Geopolitical & Macroeconomic Risks:** Heightened tensions in the Middle East, and uncertainties about a global economic slowdown, could hinder international travel demand.
- **High Valuation:** Shares currently trade at approximately 27x forward earnings, above historical and sector averages, which could limit further upside or amplify reactions to negative news.
- **Franchise Quality Assurance:** With rapid expansion, maintaining consistent brand and service standards with franchisees remains a critical challenge.

## Notable Recent News (Spring 2024)
- **Expansion in India**: Marriott plans to add 100+ hotels in India by 2027, aiming to double its footprint in one of the world’s fastest-growing travel markets.
- **AI-powered Guest Services**: Announced new investments in AI tech for customer service, room personalization, and operational efficiency.
- **Sustainability Initiatives**: Increased focus on reducing carbon intensity, with a goal of 50% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2030 (as per new ESG disclosure).

## Summary Sentiment Analysis

**Overall Sentiment:** Mildly Positive

- **Positives**: Strong financial momentum post-pandemic, effective growth and loyalty strategies, and sound execution in asset-light franchising.
- **Negatives**: Potential headwinds from cost pressures, international exposures (especially China), and a premium valuation.

**Investment Conclusion:**  
Marriott International remains a leader in the global lodging industry with favorable growth drivers, but investors should monitor macro risks and signs of slower economic or travel trends.

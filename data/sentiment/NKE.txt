# NIKE, Inc. (NKE) Company Report  
**Data collected as of June 24, 2024**

## Overview

NIKE, Inc. is a leading designer, marketer, and distributor of athletic footwear, apparel, equipment, and accessories for sports and everyday use, operating globally with major market presence in North America, EMEA, Greater China, and Asia-Pacific/Latin America.

---

## 1. Recent Financial Performance

### Q3 FY2024 Highlights (ended February 29, 2024)
- **Revenue:** $12.4 billion (flat year-over-year)
- **Net Income:** $1.2 billion (down 5% year-over-year)
- **Earnings per Share (EPS):** $0.77 (vs analyst expectation of $0.74)
- **Gross Margin:** 44.8% (up 140 bps YoY)
- **Cash and Equivalents:** $10.6 billion (up vs previous year)

### Market Reaction
- As of June 2024, NKE stock trades near $96/share, down about 10% year-to-date and underperforming the S&P 500 index.
- The stock faced downward pressure after management issued conservative guidance for Q4 and FY2025, citing “uneven consumer demand,” especially in Greater China.

---

## 2. Key Insights

### Positive Highlights

- **Brand Strength & Innovation:** NIKE maintains the #1 sports brand position globally, driven by innovation in footwear and apparel (recent launches: Pegasus 41, new Air Max lines).
- **Direct-to-Consumer Boost:** Digital and direct sales now represent over 45% of revenue, improving margins and customer data collection.
- **Operating Efficiency:** Aggressive cost control measures raised gross margin and helped offset flat revenue. Inventory management improved, reducing excess stock problems from 2023.
- **Sustainability & ESG:** Continued investment in sustainable materials/products, and progress on DEI (Diversity, Equity & Inclusion) initiatives, which bolsters corporate reputation.
  
### Negative Highlights

- **Growth Challenges:** Revenue growth is flat, with weakness in North America (-3%) offsetting modest gains elsewhere; management expects low-single-digit growth at best in near-term.
- **China Slowdown:** Greater China sales have stagnated amid consumer caution and intensified competition from local brands like Anta and Li Ning.
- **Wholesale Partner Pressures:** Some retail partners are ordering cautiously due to macroeconomic uncertainties and inventory normalization.
- **Cost Pressures:** While gross margin improved, continued inflation and currency headwinds could hurt profits going forward.

---

## 3. Recent News & Initiatives

- **Restructuring Plan:** Announced in late 2023, targeted $2 billion in cost savings over the next 3 years. Included streamlining product portfolio and reducing workforce.
- **Leadership Change:** CFO Matt Friend elevated to a broader operating role, indicating sharper focus on efficiency and digital transformation.
- **Legal & Regulatory:** Ongoing litigation regarding employee rights, as well as ongoing trademark disputes with major rivals, though these are considered “normal course” for a company of this size.

---

## 4. Sentiment Analysis

**Overall Sentiment: Neutral-to-Slightly Negative**  
While NIKE remains an iconic brand with a strong financial base, it is facing several headwinds that are dampening growth prospects in the near-term. Management is taking steps to adapt, but investors appear cautious, judging by the underperformance of the stock.

- **Positives:** Brand power, improving margin, strong cash position, sustainability leadership.
- **Negatives:** Flat/declining revenue, China competition, macroeconomic headwinds, near-term growth uncertainty.

---

## 5. Conclusion

NIKE is weathering a period of slower sales amid a dynamic and competitive global landscape. Its strong balance sheet and brand equity provide a foundation for long-term success, but investors should expect modest growth and continued volatility in the near future.
